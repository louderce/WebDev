Let's learn what is means to braise!

So, fun story, I used to thingk that to braise and to sear were the same thing. Both techniques use high heat, and both result in your food taking on a beautiful brown color on the outside. Braising starts with a sear, but finishes with the food simmering in juice along with other seasonings and aromatics to bring out the best flavors of your food. You will want to make sure you use a deepr pan, and if braising a largr portion of meat such as ribs, you will want a lid

Since braising starts with a sear, you will add any salt or spices such as black pepper directly to your food. Herbs will go in the braising liquid and their flavor will continue to develop throughout the rest of the cooking process.

Lets get some supplies!

  • Deep cooking pan with lid
  • High smoke-point oil
  • Preference of seasonings
  • Broth, water or wine
  • Food of choice
