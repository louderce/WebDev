Let's learn how to poach and egg!

Poach is a fancy way of saying to boily gently in water. Eggs aren't the only food that can be poached, you may also encounter poached fish or chicken in recipies from time to time. Because eggs are the most common, I chose to focus on those. The key to a successful poached egg is to boil it gently, if the water is boiling too rapidly, you risk breaking the delecate yolk. Also, you only need enough water to cover the egg. There is no benifit to cracking an egg into a full pot of water; its simply not necessary and may just making things more diffucult than they need to be

All you need is a pot of hot water brought to a gentile boil -just a bove a simmer, and an egg. Some people like to add a tablespoon or two of vinegar to the water help keep the egg together, but honestly I've never had that issue. Note, cracking a cold egg into a pot of water will bring the temperature of the water down momentarily. It is not recommended to poach more than 4 eggs at a time for beginners.

Let's grab some supplies:

  • Pot of water
  • an egg
  • A larger spoon to remove the egg from the water
